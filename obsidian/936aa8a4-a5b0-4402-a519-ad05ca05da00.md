Unit testing can find anywhere from 10 to 50 percent of the defects in a program. System testing can find from 20 to 60 percent of a program's defects. Together, their cumulative defect-detection rate is often less than 60 percent (Jones 1986a). The remaining errors are found either by other error-detecting techniques such as reviews or by end-users after the software has been put into production.
